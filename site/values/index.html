In the graph — your likely high-value reading list

On regulated capitalism / strong public sector

  • polanyi-karl — "fictitious commodities" and the "double movement"; the cleanest theoretical underwriting of "capitalism but regulated."
  • keynes-jm, welfare-state — the mid-century settlement.
  • galbraith-john-kenneth — The Affluent Society, countervailing power, "private affluence and public squalor"; the great American Keynesian-institutionalist voice.
  • minsky-hyman — financial instability hypothesis; why unregulated finance is inherently unstable.
  • robinson-joan — Cambridge Keynesian; the most important woman economist of the 20th century.
  • krugman-paul — the contemporary Keynesian public voice; NYT columnist for 25 years.
  • stiglitz-joseph — information asymmetries and the critique of the Washington Consensus.
  • mazzucato-mariana — the entrepreneurial state; mission-oriented industrial policy.
  • piketty-thomas — empirical inequality, r > g, progressive wealth taxation.
  • putnam-robert — social capital and the civic preconditions of democratic capacity.
  • jacobs-jane — cities, public space, and the democratic conditions of community.
  • harvey-david, klein-naomi, giridharadas-anand — the contemporary case against unregulated capital.
  • anderson-elizabeth — Private Government: the workplace as unaccountable authoritarian state.

On voice to the voiceless / unexamined privilege

  • weil-simone — attention as the central moral discipline.
  • sen-amartya, nussbaum-martha — the capability approach.
  • ellison-ralph, baldwin-james, cobb-jelani, morrison-toni — the tradition of registering what dominant eyes won't see.
  • wollstonecraft-mary, de-beauvoir-simone, woolf-virginia, hooks-bell, lorde-audre, young-iris-marion, intersectionality — feminist thought across generations.
  • ehrenreich-barbara, abramsky-sasha — attention to working-class and poor American lives.
  • wells-ida-b, du-bois-web — turn-of-the-century empirical documentation of American terror.

On Sermon-on-the-Mount Christianity / critique of White Christianity / Black Christian resonance

  • day-dorothy, merton-thomas — two different ways of taking the Sermon literally.
  • bonhoeffer-dietrich — "cheap grace" is almost exactly your diagnosis of White American Christianity.
  • thurman-howard — Jesus and the Disinherited; the theological heart of the civil-rights-movement.
  • king-mlk — the central bridge between Black Christianity and public action; see also beloved-community, nonviolence.
  • murray-pauli — civil-rights lawyer, feminist, Episcopal priest; at the crossroads of nearly every value you name.
  • stevenson-bryan — Christian-rooted justice advocacy in direct Sermon-on-the-Mount lineage.
  • berry-wendell — lay Baptist agrarian; neighborly, Sermon-grounded, anti-industrial.
  • niebuhr-reinhold — Christian realism; "good and evil intertwined."
  • tillich-paul — "the Protestant principle": no finite form captures the infinite.
  • social-gospel, liberation-theology — the schools.

On good and evil intertwined / moral complexity

  • niebuhr-reinhold; o-connor-flannery (grace as violent intrusion); arendt-hannah (banality of evil); berlin-isaiah (value pluralism); williams-bernard (against moral systematizing).

On "racism/colonialism baked into capitalism"

  • robinson-cedric — Black Marxism; the racial-capitalism thesis.
  • du-bois-web — Black Reconstruction, the empirical groundwork.
  • wilkerson-isabel — caste, and the Great Migration as American history.
  • davis-angela — the prison-industrial complex and its abolition.
  • forman-james-jr — the complicated political construction of mass-incarceration.

Liberal political philosophy (for orientation, your sympathies and opposition)
