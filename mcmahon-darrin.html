Darrin M. McMahon

1965 – ? · American
#intellectual-history#history-of-ideas#enlightenment#equality

American intellectual historian, David Herbert Donald Chair in History at Dartmouth, and one of the better practitioners of the conceptual-history genre — book-length histories of a single big idea traced from antiquity through the present. McMahon trained at Yale, taught for years at Florida State, and has edited, with Samuel Moyn, Rethinking Modern European Intellectual History (2014), a useful survey of where the field stands.

Four books define his career. Enemies of the Enlightenment (2001) — his dissertation book — recovered the French Counter-Enlightenment as a serious intellectual movement rather than a reactionary embarrassment, and remains the standard work on the subject. Happiness: A History (2006) is the book that made his reputation: a cultural history of how Western thought moved from treating happiness as eudaimonia or rare fortune toward treating it as an entitlement and even an obligation, with the American Declaration's "pursuit of Happiness" as one hinge of the story. Divine Fury: A History of Genius (2013) traces the cultural-religious career of "genius" as a category, from classical daimons through Romantic cults of the great man to its later democratization and eventual neurological reduction.

Equality: The History of an Elusive Idea (2023) is his most recent and, for readers of this graph, the most directly relevant — a natural companion to David Lay Williams's The Greatest of All Plagues: Williams asks what canonical thinkers said about inequality; McMahon asks what the positive concept of equality has meant across 2,500 years, and how unstable and contested it has always been. Together the two books supply the intellectual-history backbone of the contemporary inequality debate.

Key ideas

  • Conceptual history as method — the career of a single idea across centuries
  • Happiness as a modern entitlement rather than classical achievement
  • "Genius" as a cultural-religious category, not a natural kind
  • Equality as chronically elusive — contested at every period
