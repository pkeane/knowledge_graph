# Recommended Reading

A curated selection of widely-admired works that together give a sense of the range of thought covered by this knowledge graph — political economy, moral and religious life, the Black American tradition, feminism, the non-Western critique of empire, modernist fiction, and the design of cities. Most are short; several are long but foundational. Each links to the author's entry.

## Foundations of modern political and moral thought

- **[[du-bois-web|W.E.B. Du Bois]], *The Souls of Black Folk*** (1903) — fourteen essays blending sociology, memoir, and lyric meditation; the starting point of Black American political thought.
- **[[weber-max|Max Weber]], *The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism*** (1905) — the canonical account of how a religious ethic gave modern capitalism its inner discipline.
- **[[keynes-jm|John Maynard Keynes]], *The General Theory of Employment, Interest, and Money*** (1936) — the book that reconstituted macroeconomics around aggregate demand and the possibility of involuntary unemployment.
- **[[arendt-hannah|Hannah Arendt]], *The Origins of Totalitarianism*** (1951) — the major 20th-century study of how anti-Semitism, imperialism, and mass politics produced a new form of rule.
- **[[niebuhr-reinhold|Reinhold Niebuhr]], *The Irony of American History*** (1952) — the canonical Christian-realist statement on good and evil intertwined in national life.
- **[[rawls-john|John Rawls]], *A Theory of Justice*** (1971) — the book that revived Anglo-American political philosophy; justice as fairness derived from the original position.

## The Black American tradition

- **[[thurman-howard|Howard Thurman]], *Jesus and the Disinherited*** (1949) — under 100 pages; a reading of the gospel from the standpoint of "the disinherited." Reportedly carried by King on the road.
- **[[ellison-ralph|Ralph Ellison]], *Invisible Man*** (1952) — mid-century American masterpiece; the unnamed narrator's picaresque from the South through Harlem.
- **[[baldwin-james|James Baldwin]], *The Fire Next Time*** (1963) — two essays, ~120 pages; the most durable postwar statement on race and American Christianity.
- **[[morrison-toni|Toni Morrison]], *Beloved*** (1987) — Pulitzer; the major American novel of the afterlives of slavery, built around the historical case of Margaret Garner.
- **[[stevenson-bryan|Bryan Stevenson]], *Just Mercy*** (2014) — the indispensable contemporary book on the American carceral state, built around the Walter McMillian case.
- **[[wilkerson-isabel|Isabel Wilkerson]], *The Warmth of Other Suns*** (2010) — fifteen years of reporting on the Great Migration through three life stories.

## Feminism and moral philosophy

- **[[de-beauvoir-simone|Simone de Beauvoir]], *The Second Sex*** (1949) — the book that established the modern philosophical study of gender; existentialist in method, historical and phenomenological in range.
- **[[berlin-isaiah|Isaiah Berlin]], *Four Essays on Liberty*** (1969) — includes "Two Concepts of Liberty"; the classic mid-century defense of value pluralism and negative freedom.
- **[[anderson-elizabeth|Elizabeth Anderson]], *Private Government*** (2017) — ~100 pages; argues that the modern workplace is *de facto* authoritarian and that classical liberalism, properly understood, ought to be horrified.

## The non-Western critique of empire

- **[[fanon-frantz|Frantz Fanon]], *The Wretched of the Earth*** (1961) — the canonical anti-colonial text; psychiatry, politics, and violence read together.
- **[[said-edward|Edward Said]], *Orientalism*** (1978) — the book that named and mapped the Western discursive construction of "the East."
- **[[sen-amartya|Amartya Sen]], *Development as Freedom*** (1999) — development reconceived as the expansion of substantive human capabilities rather than GDP growth.

## Existentialism and the religious imagination

- **[[camus-albert|Albert Camus]], *The Plague*** (1947) — decency tested, unsentimentally and without theological consolation, under conditions that admit no triumph.
- **[[percy-walker|Walker Percy]], *The Moviegoer*** (1961) — National Book Award; a New Orleans stockbroker in what Percy called "everydayness," undertaking a quiet Kierkegaardian search.
- **[[o-connor-flannery|Flannery O'Connor]], *A Good Man Is Hard to Find*** (1955) — short stories; O'Connor's Catholic imagination in its compressed, violent form.

## Cities, work, and the built environment

- **[[jacobs-jane|Jane Jacobs]], *The Death and Life of Great American Cities*** (1961) — the book that ended the authority of orthodox urban-renewal planning and reframed the sidewalk as the organ of urban safety.
- **[[berry-wendell|Wendell Berry]], *Hannah Coulter*** (2004) — short novel; agrarian Christianity in narrative form.

## Inequality and the present

- **[[piketty-thomas|Thomas Piketty]], *Capital in the Twenty-First Century*** (2014) — the empirical spine of the contemporary inequality debate; the introduction and Part IV alone repay the effort.
- **[[coates-ta-nehisi|Ta-Nehisi Coates]], *Between the World and Me*** (2015) — a letter to his son; the most widely read contemporary statement in the Baldwin lineage.
