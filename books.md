# A Reading List

Books that match the values lens of this knowledge graph and (where possible) connect to nodes already in it. A mix of essential classics you may not have read and recent works that synthesize the past well.

## Six classics, each short

1. **[[thurman-howard|Howard Thurman]], *Jesus and the Disinherited*** (1949) — under 100 pages. The single book that maps closest to your VALUES; MLK reportedly carried it on the road. If you read only one thing on this list, this one.
2. **[[du-bois-web|W.E.B. Du Bois]], *The Souls of Black Folk*** (1903) — fourteen essays mixing sociology, memoir, and lyric meditation. Foundation of American Black political thought; the chapter on the sorrow songs is a music essay you'll never forget.
3. **[[baldwin-james|James Baldwin]], *The Fire Next Time*** (1963) — two essays, ~120 pages. Devastatingly clear on race and on what White American Christianity has refused to see. Reads in one sitting.
4. **[[niebuhr-reinhold|Reinhold Niebuhr]], *The Irony of American History*** (1952) — the canonical Christian-realist statement on good and evil intertwined. Short, hard, durable.
5. **[[berry-wendell|Wendell Berry]], *Hannah Coulter*** (2004) — short novel. Berry's agrarian Christianity in narrative rather than essay form; quieter than the essays and easier to enter through.
6. **Marilynne Robinson, *Gilead*** (2004) — Pulitzer; a small American masterpiece. An aging Iowa Congregationalist preacher writing to his young son. Calvinist humanism rendered as fiction. *(Robinson is on the wishlist but not yet in the graph.)*

## Five recent works that synthesize well

7. **[[stevenson-bryan|Bryan Stevenson]], *Just Mercy*** (2014) — the indispensable contemporary book on the American carceral state. Built around the Walter McMillian case; in a Sermon-on-the-Mount lineage but by way of Alabama death row.
8. **[[wilkerson-isabel|Isabel Wilkerson]], *The Warmth of Other Suns*** (2010) — fifteen years of reporting on the Great Migration through three life stories. One of the best American nonfiction books of the century, on a subject most American history misses.
9. **[[mazzucato-mariana|Mariana Mazzucato]], *The Entrepreneurial State*** (2013) — short and polemical. Will permanently change how you read the Silicon Valley story.
10. **[[anderson-elizabeth|Elizabeth Anderson]], *Private Government*** (2017) — about 100 pages of lectures plus respondents. The argument that the modern American workplace is *de facto* authoritarian and that classical liberalism, properly understood, ought to be horrified.
11. **Heather McGhee, *The Sum of Us*** (2021) — accessible, vivid, the single best contemporary book on how racism costs everyone (the "drained pool" framing). Reads like long-form journalism; argues like serious political analysis. *(McGhee is not yet in the graph.)*

## One wildcard worth your attention

12. **Adam Tooze, *Crashed*** (2018) — 700 pages on the 2008 financial crisis and the decade after; the most ambitious recent attempt to write the political economy of crisis as a single coherent story. Hard going but worth it; it's the book to read after [[stiglitz-joseph|Stiglitz]], [[krugman-paul|Krugman]], and [[minsky-hyman|Minsky]] to see how the crisis actually unfolded across the world economy. *(Tooze is not yet in the graph.)*

## A one-month plan

If you want a focused start: Thurman → Baldwin → Niebuhr → Stevenson → McGhee. Five short books, total under a thousand pages, that together rehearse most of your VALUES.md from inside the traditions that hold them.
